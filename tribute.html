Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

" Thinking should become your capital asset, no matter whatever ups and downs you come across in your life."

From humble origins to 'People's President' - Epic Journey of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from from St. Joseph's in Trichy.
  • 1958 - Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1960 - He joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation. Five years later he joined the Indian Space Research Organisation.
  • 1980 - Rohini Satellite Launched. Dr. Kalam made significant contribution as Project Director to develop India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully injected the Rohini satellite in the near earth orbit in July 1980 and made India an exclusive member of Space Club.
  • 1981 - Awarded Padma Bhushan.
  • 1982 - He joinned the Defence Research and Development Laboratory in Hyderabad as director after his stint with the Vikram Sarabbai Space Centre.
  • 1987 - Om Prakash Bhasin awards for science and technology for 1986.
  • 1989 - Indian scientists of the DRDO successfully test fired the Agni missile into the waters of the Indian Ocean. He was responsible for the development and operationalisation of AGNI.
  • 1989 - Speaking on the 'Agni Mission' he said India's successful testing of the 'Agni' missile signified its expertise in the rarefied hi-tech areas of heat-shield technology.
  • 1990 - Awarded Padma Vibhushan.Lok Sabha congratulated the Indian scientists team headed by Dr A. P. J. Abdul Kalam for the successful launch of the "Akash" multi-target surface-to-air missile.
  • 1992 - Chief Advisor for the India's Nuclear Program.
  • 1998 - Awarded Bharat Ratna and IG Award.
  • 2002 - Became 11th President of India.
  • 2015 - The People's President passes away while doing what he loved the most, addressing students.
